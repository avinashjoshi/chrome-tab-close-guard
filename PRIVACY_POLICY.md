# Privacy Policy for Tab Close Guard

**Last Updated:** December 4, 2024

## Overview

Tab Close Guard is committed to protecting your privacy. This privacy policy explains how the extension handles your data.

## Data Collection

**Tab Close Guard does NOT collect, store, or transmit ANY personal data.**

Specifically, we do not:
- Collect browsing history
- Track your activity
- Store any personal information
- Send data to external servers
- Use analytics or tracking tools
- Share data with third parties

## Data Storage

All extension settings are stored **locally on your device** using Chrome's built-in storage API (`chrome.storage.sync`). This includes:
- List of websites you've added for protection
- "Block on all websites" preference
- "App/PWA mode only" settings for each site

This data:
- Remains on your device
- Syncs across your Chrome browsers if you're signed into Chrome (handled by Google, not us)
- Can be deleted at any time by removing the extension or clearing extension data

## Permissions

The extension requires these permissions:
- **storage**: To save your site preferences locally
- **activeTab**: To read the current tab's URL when you click "Add Current" button (not used for any other purpose)

If we update this privacy policy, we will post the new policy here with an updated "Last Updated" date.
